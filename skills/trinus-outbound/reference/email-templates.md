# Email Templates — Trinus Outbound

## Core Philosophy

**If the same email could be sent to 20 other IT consulting firms, it fails.**

Trinus emails work because they:
1. Reference a specific, real event at the prospect's company
2. Connect that event to a specific data/IT pain that Trinus solves
3. Ask a question that makes the prospect think — not sell them anything
4. Never mention "digital transformation", "leverage AI", or "innovative solutions"

**Sender identity:** Ask the user for their name on the first run. Use it consistently. Trinus has 25+ years of enterprise client history — let that credibility come through in specificity and tone, not boasting.

## THE MASTER TEMPLATE (all 5 emails)

### Email 1 — Day 0 (Initial Outreach)

**Target word count: 60-85 words**

**With signal:**
```
Hey [First Name],

Saw [specific signal — one sentence, factual].

[Business reality implication — what that usually means for their data/IT environment — one sentence].

Quick question — [specific question about their current data/IT capacity, tied to their industry and signal]?

We work with [relevant industry] companies like [relevant Trinus client if applicable — Amgen, AT&T, BofA, Intel, etc.] on [specific service]. Happy to share a few examples if relevant.

– [Sender Name]
[Title], Trinus
```

**Without signal (fallback):**
```
Hey [First Name],

[Company] came up while I was looking at [industry] companies dealing with [specific pain area].

Quick question — [question about their data/IT capacity, specific to their world]?

We work with [industry] companies — [relevant client example] — on [specific service]. Happy to share a few examples if it's relevant.

– [Sender Name]
[Title], Trinus
```

## Personalization Rules by Signal Type

### M&A Signal

**Email 1 pattern:**
```
Saw [Company A] acquired [Company B] [timeframe].

Post-merger, most [industry] companies find that the data layer is the first thing that needs reconciliation — two systems, two reporting environments, two definitions of the same metric.

Quick question — how is the [Company] team thinking about the integration on the analytics/data side?

We've worked with companies through similar transitions — [relevant client if applicable]. Happy to share what that looked like.
```

**Key:** Never assume chaos or problems. Frame as "the challenge most companies face" — respectful, not presumptuous.

---

### New CDO / CTO / CIO Hired

**Email 1 pattern:**
```
Saw [Name] just joined [Company] as [Title].

New data leaders usually have a narrow window to define their roadmap before the organization locks in expectations.

Quick question — is [Company] working through a data infrastructure assessment right now, or is [Name] inheriting an existing roadmap?

We work with [industry] companies on [specific service]. Happy to share a few examples of how peers have approached this.
```

**Key:** Reference the leader by name. This shows you read the news — not just pulled a list.

---

### Cloud Migration / Digital Transformation

**Email 1 pattern:**
```
Saw [Company]'s [cloud migration / digital transformation] initiative [brief detail].

In [industry], the cloud migration usually surfaces data pipeline complexity that wasn't obvious from the infrastructure side.

Quick question — how is the [Company] team handling the data engineering piece — is it in-house, or are you working with partners on that layer?

We've helped [industry] companies — [client example] — through similar migrations. Happy to share what we did.
```

---

### Hiring Spike

**Email 1 pattern:**
```
Saw [Company] is hiring [X] data engineers / cloud architects / BI developers right now.

That usually means the roadmap is moving faster than internal capacity can keep up — especially for [their specific tech stack or industry context].

Quick question — is the current search for permanent hires, or is there a contractor / augmentation path that would get things moving sooner?

We staff data and cloud talent specifically for [industry] companies. Happy to share how we work.
```

### Compliance Pressure

**Email 1 pattern (Healthcare / Life Sciences):**
```
Saw [Company] is navigating [FDA / HIPAA / SOX] [context — audit, new requirement, etc.].

For [healthcare / pharma / financial] companies, compliance timelines tend to compress quickly and expose gaps in data lineage and governance tooling.

Quick question — how is the [Company] team handling the data governance layer right now — is that something the internal team owns, or are you working with external partners on it?

We work with companies like [Amgen / J&J / KPMG] on exactly this. Happy to share a few examples.
```

## Hard Rules

1. **Each sentence = its own paragraph.** No dense blocks.
2. **Email 1 must be 60-85 words.** If over 85, cut. If under 60, it's too thin — add specificity.
3. **Only two things change per contact:** The signal line and the question. Everything else is templated.
4. **Never pitch.** The question implies the problem. The client reference implies the solution.
5. **Never say:** "digital transformation", "leverage AI", "innovative solutions", "cutting-edge", "streamline", "synergies", "holistic approach"
6. **Always use a real Trinus client name** if it matches the prospect's industry. The proof is the roster.
7. **Trinus MBE/WBE certification** — only mention if the prospect is in Government/Utilities procurement OR if they mention supplier diversity. Never lead with it.
8. **DO NOT pitch all services.** Pick ONE service that maps to the detected signal. Stay focused.

## Quality Checklist (run before finalizing any Email 1)

- [ ] Does Email 1 reference a specific, real signal (not "I saw your website")?
- [ ] Does the question connect to their specific industry and buying context?
- [ ] Is it 60-85 words?
- [ ] Each sentence its own paragraph?
- [ ] No generic IT consulting language?
- [ ] Client reference matches prospect's industry?
- [ ] Only ONE service angle (not a menu of services)?
- [ ] No pitching — just signal + question + proof?

If any item fails, rewrite before sending.
